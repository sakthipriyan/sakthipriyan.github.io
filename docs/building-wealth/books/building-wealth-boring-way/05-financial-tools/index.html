Chapter 5

Financial Tools for Building Wealth

Your Complete Financial Toolkit

Overview

Building wealth requires the right tools. Just as a craftsman needs quality instruments, you need appropriate financial tools‚Äîeach serving a specific purpose in your wealth-building journey. This chapter provides a comprehensive overview of the financial tools available and how to use them effectively.

1. Cash & Bank Accounts

Purpose: Liquidity and easy access üíß

Bank Accounts

Status: üü¢ Continue
Purpose: Salary credit, bill payments, and daily expenses

Why Essential:

  • Primary interface with the financial system
  • Enables UPI, NEFT, RTGS transfers
  • Debit card facility
  • Free banking services
  • Safe and insured up to ‚Çπ5 lakhs per bank (DICGC)

Best Practices:

  • Maintain 2-3 accounts for different purposes
  • Keep minimum balance to avoid fees
  • Use zero-balance accounts where possible
  • Enable mobile banking and alerts

Cash in Hand

Status: üü° Minimal
Purpose: Limited non-digital usage

When Needed:

  • Small vendors without digital payment
  • Emergency situations
  • Places without network connectivity

Recommendation: Keep ‚Çπ2,000-5,000 for emergencies; rely primarily on digital payments

Fixed Deposits (FD) / Recurring Deposits (RD)

Status: üî¥ Exited
Purpose: Previously used, now very tax inefficient

Why Not Recommended:

  • Interest taxed at your slab rate (up to 30%)
  • Returns barely beat inflation post-tax
  • Better alternatives available (debt funds)
  • Lock-in reduces flexibility
  • Premature withdrawal penalties

Exception: Short-term FDs (< 1 year) for specific goals if needed

2. Insurance

Purpose: Risk management, not returns üõ°Ô∏è

Important Principle: Insurance is your first line of defense for building wealth. It protects you from financial catastrophes that could destroy years of wealth accumulation.

Health Insurance

Status: üü¢ Continue
Purpose: Covers hospitalization & health-related expenses

Coverage Needed:

  • Individual: ‚Çπ10-15 lakhs
  • Family: ‚Çπ25-50 lakhs
  • Super top-up: Additional ‚Çπ50 lakhs-1 crore

Why Critical:

  • Medical inflation ~15% annually
  • A single hospitalization can wipe out savings
  • Cashless treatment facility
  • Tax benefits under 80D

Tips:

  • Buy early when premiums are low
  • Prefer individual policies over family floater for better coverage
  • Include critical illness rider
  • Check hospital network and claim settlement ratio

Term Life / Accident Insurance

Status: üü¢ Continue
Purpose: Protects dependents in case of untimely demise

Coverage Needed:

  • 10-15x of annual income
  • Sufficient to replace lost income for family

Why Essential:

  • Pure protection, no investment component
  • Very affordable (‚Çπ500-1,000/month for ‚Çπ1 crore cover)
  • Financial security for dependents
  • Loan protection

What to Avoid:

  • Endowment policies (low returns, high costs)
  • Money-back policies
  • ULIPs (if bought after 2010)

Motor Insurance

Status: üü¢ Continue
Purpose: Mandatory for all vehicles

Types:

  • Third-party: Mandatory by law
  • Comprehensive: Recommended (own damage + third-party)

Tips:

  • Compare quotes online annually
  • Increase deductible to reduce premium
  • No Claim Bonus accumulation
  • Avoid unnecessary add-ons

Home Insurance

Status: üü° Optional
Purpose: Protects property & belongings

When Recommended:

  • Own a house worth significant amount
  • Live in areas prone to natural disasters
  • Have expensive contents/belongings

What It Covers:

  • Structure damage
  • Contents theft/damage
  • Fire, flood, earthquake
  • Third-party liability

3. Investments

Purpose: üìà Long-term growth for building wealth

This is where actual wealth creation happens through systematic investing in productive assets.

3.A. Equity

Primary Purpose: Grow wealth by owning businesses

Index Funds

Status: üü¢ Continue
Why: Low-cost, broad-market exposure

Recommended Allocation: 60-80% of equity portfolio

Types:

  • Nifty 50: Large-cap stability
  • Nifty Next 50: Emerging large-caps
  • Midcap 150: Growth potential
  • Smallcap 250: Long-term compounding

Advantages:

  • Very low expense ratios (0.05-0.20%)
  • No fund manager risk
  • Broad diversification
  • Transparent holdings

Active Mutual Funds

Status: üü° Paused
Why: Higher cost, inconsistent performance

Reality Check:

  • Only 10-15% of active funds beat index over 10+ years
  • Higher expense ratios (1-2%)
  • Tax inefficiency due to frequent churning
  • Manager-dependent performance

When to Consider:

  • Specific sector/theme exposure
  • Proven fund manager with 10+ year track record

Direct Equity (Demat Account)

Status: üü° Paused
Why: Requires significant time and expertise

Who Should Consider:

  • Experienced investors
  • Those who can dedicate time to research
  • Want complete control over holdings

Why Most Should Avoid:

  • High risk for beginners
  • Time-intensive research needed
  • Emotional decision-making risk
  • Difficult to maintain discipline

US Equity (International Broker)

Status: üü¢ Continue
Why: Global diversification and currency hedge

Allocation: 20-40% of equity portfolio

How to Access:

  • Direct international broker (IBKR, etc.)
  • NASDAQ 100 ETF (UCITS, Irish-domiciled)
  • Benefits from USD appreciation vs INR

Advantages:

  • Geographical diversification
  • Currency hedge (USD vs INR)
  • Access to global leaders (Apple, Microsoft, Google)
  • 20+ year CAGR of 24% (in INR terms)

3.B. Hybrid & Debt

Purpose: Steady income and lower volatility üí∞

Arbitrage Funds

Status: üü¢ Continue
Purpose: Short-term parking for travel/medical corpus

Characteristics:

  • Low risk, low volatility
  • 7-8% returns
  • Taxed as equity (advantageous)
  • 3-12 month holding period ideal

Use Cases:

  • Travel fund corpus
  • Medical fund corpus
  • Short-term goals (<1 year)
  • Parking money between investments

Debt Mutual Funds

Status: üü° Paused
Why: Reduced benefit after Apr 2023 taxation change

What Changed:

  • All debt funds now taxed at slab rate
  • No indexation benefit for LTCG
  • Made less attractive vs earlier

Types (for reference):

  • Liquid Funds: Best for emergency fund
  • Ultra Short/Short Duration: 1-3 year goals
  • Dynamic Bond: Interest rate management
  • Credit Risk: Higher yield, higher risk

Conservative Hybrid Funds

Status: üü° Paused (for new investments)
Existing Holdings: Continue

Allocation: 75-90% debt, 10-25% equity

Best For:

  • Emergency fund (middle portion)
  • 2-3 year goals
  • Conservative investors

Returns: 9-10% historically

3.C. Retirement-Focused

Purpose: Long-term stability and retirement-focused growth üí°

Public Provident Fund (PPF)

Status: üü¢ Continue
Why: Long-term, government-backed, tax-efficient savings

Key Features:

  • 15-year lock-in (extendable)
  • Current interest: ~7.1% (government decides)
  • EEE status (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt)
  • Maximum: ‚Çπ1.5 lakhs/year

Why Continue:

  • Absolutely safe
  • Tax-free returns
  • Disciplined long-term savings
  • Debt allocation in portfolio

Limitations:

  • Low liquidity (partial withdrawal after 7 years)
  • Interest rate risk (government changes rates)
  • Limited annual contribution

Employee Provident Fund (EPF)

Status: üü° Minimum
Why: Mandatory for salaried; offers tax-free compounding

Features:

  • 12% employee + 12% employer contribution
  • Current interest: ~8.25%
  • Tax-free returns (conditions apply)
  • Retirement-focused

Strategy:

  • Mandatory contribution only
  • Avoid VPF (Voluntary PF) due to liquidity lock
  • Prefer PPF for additional debt allocation

National Pension System (NPS)

Status: üü° Minimum
Why: Limited flexibility and withdrawal constraints

Features:

  • Market-linked returns
  • Additional ‚Çπ50,000 tax deduction (80CCD(1B))
  • Partial equity exposure allowed
  • Very low cost (0.01% fund management)

Limitations:

  • Lock-in till age 60
  • Only 60% withdrawal allowed at retirement
  • 40% must buy annuity
  • Inflexible structure

When to Consider:

  • Already maxed out 80C limit
  • Want additional tax deduction
  • Comfortable with lock-in

3.D. Gold

Gold ETF

  • Intraday liquidity
  • Very low expense ratio (0.05-0.20%)

Gold Mutual Fund

Features:

  • No demat needed
  • Slightly higher cost than ETF
  • SIP facility available

Allocation: 5-10% of portfolio

Physical Gold

Status: üü° Minimal
Purpose: Jewellery as required, not investment

Why Not Investment:

  • High making charges (8-20%)
  • Purity concerns
  • Storage risk
  • Buy-sell spread

When to Buy:

  • Jewellery for personal use
  • Cultural/traditional requirements
  • Not as investment vehicle

4. Credit Cards & Loans

Purpose: Smart leverage and cash-flow management üí≥

Credit Cards

Status: üü¢ Continue
Why: Rewards, convenience, and short-term credit

Benefits:

  • 30-45 day interest-free credit
  • Reward points and cashback (2-5%)
  • Better fraud protection vs debit cards
  • Expense tracking and statements
  • Purchase protection and insurance

Best Practices:

  • Always pay full amount before due date
  • Never revolve credit (20-40% interest rate!)
  • Use for planned expenses only
  • Track spending across all cards
  • Optimize for category rewards

Strategy:

  • Different cards for different purposes
  • Food card for dining/groceries
  • Travel card for trips
  • Online card for e-commerce
  • General expense card for utilities

Loans

Status: üü° Minimal
Why: Avoid for consumption; consider only for appreciating assets

Good Debt (Consider carefully):

  • Home Loan: For primary residence
    • Tax benefits on principal (80C) and interest (24)
    • Builds equity over time
    • Lower interest (7-9%)
  • Education Loan: For skill-building
    • Investment in earning potential
    • Tax benefits on interest

Bad Debt (Avoid):

  • Personal Loan: 10-18% interest
  • Car Loan: Depreciating asset
  • Consumer Durables Loan: Lifestyle inflation
  • Credit Card Debt: 20-40% interest

Golden Rule: Use credit, but don’t let it use you

Creating Your Financial Tools Strategy

Phase 1: Foundation (First 2 years)

Priority Order:

  1. Open necessary bank accounts
  2. Buy term insurance
  3. Buy health insurance
  4. Build emergency fund (liquid/arbitrage)
  5. Start PPF account

Outcome: Safety net established

Phase 2: Growth (Years 3-10)

Add to foundation:

  1. Start equity index fund SIPs
  2. Increase PPF contribution
  3. Add international equity exposure
  4. Gold allocation (5-10%)
  5. Optimize credit card rewards

Outcome: Wealth building accelerates

Phase 3: Optimization (Years 10+)

Mature strategy:

  1. Increase equity allocation (70-80%)
  2. Reduce debt allocation (15-20%)
  3. Maintain gold (5-10%)
  4. Tax optimization
  5. Estate planning

Outcome: Sustainable wealth creation

Key Principles

  1. Insurance First: Protect before you build
  2. Emergency Fund: Safety before growth
  3. Equity for Growth: 60-80% for wealth building
  4. Index Over Active: Lower cost, better consistency
  5. Global Diversification: Don’t put all eggs in one country
  6. Debt for Stability: 10-20% for volatility management
  7. Credit Smart: Use for rewards, never revolve
  8. Avoid Bad Debt: No consumption loans

Conclusion

Your financial tools should match your wealth-building phase. Start with protection and safety, then gradually add growth instruments. Keep it simple, keep it boring, keep it consistent.

The right tools don’t make wealth-building exciting‚Äîthey make it predictable, sustainable, and successful.
