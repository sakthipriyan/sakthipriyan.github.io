How to Invest in Debt for Building Wealth

Stability, safety, and strategic allocation using debt mutual funds, PPF, and arbitrage funds.

Introduction

Debt investments are often misunderstood. They’re not as exciting as equity, they don’t make headlines, and they won’t make you rich quickly. But they serve a critical role in your wealth-building journey.

The Truth: Debt is not a wealth creator‚Äîit is a wealth stabilizer and risk reducer.

What is Debt?

The Fundamental Concept

Debt means you lend your money safely to an institution and earn steady interest in return.

These institutions can include:

  • Governments: Central/State government bonds
  • Banks: Through fixed deposits, bonds
  • Companies: Corporate bonds, commercial paper

How Debt Works

Equity: You own a piece of the business ‚Üí share in profits/losses
Debt: You lend money to the business ‚Üí get fixed interest, regardless of profit/loss

Example:

  • You buy ‚Çπ1,00,000 of a 10-year government bond at 7% interest
  • Every year, you get ‚Çπ7,000 interest
  • After 10 years, you get back your ‚Çπ1,00,000
  • Total gain: ‚Çπ70,000 (predictable)

Compare with equity:

  • You buy ‚Çπ1,00,000 of shares
  • Company may profit ‚Üí shares go up
  • Company may loss ‚Üí shares go down
  • After 10 years: Could be ‚Çπ50,000 or ‚Çπ5,00,000 (unpredictable)

When Debt Shines

Scenario 1: Market Crash

  • Equity falls 30-40%
  • Debt remains stable or grows slightly
  • Your portfolio falls only 20-25% (instead of 30-40%)

Scenario 2: Near-Term Goal

  • Need money in 2 years for house down payment
  • Equity too risky (could fall)
  • Debt provides predictable growth

Scenario 3: Retirement

  • No regular income
  • Can’t afford equity volatility
  • Debt provides steady income stream

The Debt Investment Landscape

1. Bank Products

Savings Account

  • Returns: 3-4%
  • Safety: Very high (DICGC insured up to ‚Çπ5L)
  • Liquidity: Instant
  • Tax: Interest taxed at slab rate
  • Use: Daily transactions, minimal idle cash

Fixed Deposit (FD)

  • Returns: 5-7%
  • Safety: Very high (DICGC insured)
  • Liquidity: Low (penalty on premature withdrawal)
  • Tax: Interest taxed at slab rate (very inefficient)
  • Use: Avoid for investments, consider only for specific short-term goals

Recurring Deposit (RD)

  • Returns: Similar to FD
  • Process: Monthly deposits ‚Üí lump sum at maturity
  • Tax: Same as FD
  • Use: Better alternatives available (SIPs in debt funds)

Recommendation: Use bank products only for operational needs, not for wealth building.

2. Government Schemes

Public Provident Fund (PPF)

  • Tenure: 15 years (extendable in blocks of 5 years)
  • Returns: ~7.1% (government decides, changes quarterly)
  • Limit: ‚Çπ1.5 lakhs/year maximum
  • Tax: EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) - completely tax-free
  • Liquidity: Partial withdrawal after 7 years
  • Use: Long-term debt allocation, retirement planning

Why PPF is Excellent:

  • Absolutely safe (government-backed)
  • Tax-free returns (rare in debt)
  • Disciplined long-term savings
  • Ideal for conservative investors

Employee Provident Fund (EPF)

  • Nature: Mandatory for salaried (12% employee + employer)
  • Returns: ~8.25% (tax-free if conditions met)
  • Liquidity: Restricted till retirement (except specific cases)
  • Use: Mandatory contribution, avoid voluntary PF

National Pension System (NPS)

  • Nature: Market-linked retirement scheme
  • Returns: Variable (depends on asset allocation)
  • Tax: Additional ‚Çπ50,000 deduction (80CCD(1B))
  • Liquidity: Locked till age 60
  • Withdrawal: Only 60% can be withdrawn, 40% must buy annuity
  • Use: Consider only if you’ve maxed out other options

3. Debt Mutual Funds

This is where debt investing becomes strategic and tax-efficient (historically).

Important: April 2023 taxation changes made debt funds less attractive, but they still serve important purposes.

Types of Debt Mutual Funds

A. Liquid Fund

Investment Strategy: Very short-term debt (‚â§ 91 days) like T-bills, commercial paper

Characteristics:

  • Risk: Very low (minimal credit and interest rate risk)
  • Returns: 6-7% XIRR
  • Liquidity: T+0 or T+1 (instant to next day)
  • Volatility: Minimal NAV fluctuation
  • Duration: Macaulay duration ‚â§ 91 days

Best For:

  • Emergency fund (first 2 months)
  • Parking money between investments
  • Better than savings account for idle cash

Example Fund: Axis Liquid Fund (XIRR: 6.93%)

Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Very low risk
  • Quick access (T+0/T+1)
  • Better returns than savings account
  • No lock-in

Cons:

  • Returns fluctuate slightly with interest rates
  • Not meant for long-term growth
  • Lower returns vs. other debt categories
  • Taxed at slab rate (post Apr 2023)

B. Dynamic Bond Fund

Investment Strategy: Invests across short, medium, and long-term debt, adjusting based on interest rate cycles

How It Works:

  • Fund manager predicts interest rate movements
  • When rates expected to fall ‚Üí increase duration (long-term bonds)
  • When rates expected to rise ‚Üí decrease duration (short-term bonds)

Characteristics:

  • Risk: Moderate (interest rate risk)
  • Returns: 8-9% XIRR
  • Volatility: Higher than liquid funds
  • Active Management: Fund manager calls on duration

Best For:

  • 3+ year investment horizon
  • Debt allocation in core portfolio
  • Those comfortable with some NAV volatility

Example Fund: ICICI Prudential All Seasons Bond Fund (XIRR: 8.35%)

Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Higher return potential than liquid funds
  • Professional duration management
  • Can benefit from falling interest rates
  • Better than fixed deposits

Cons:

  • Interest rate timing risk
  • Higher NAV volatility
  • Not suitable for <3 year goals
  • Manager-dependent performance

C. Conservative Hybrid Fund

Investment Strategy: Hybrid fund investing 75‚Äì90% in debt and 10‚Äì25% in equity

Dual Nature:

  • Primary: Debt fund (low risk, steady returns)
  • Kicker: Small equity exposure (potential for extra returns)

Characteristics:

  • Risk: Moderate (primarily debt risk + small equity risk)
  • Returns: 9-10% XIRR
  • Volatility: Moderate (due to equity component)
  • Tax: Taxed as debt fund

Best For:

  • Emergency fund (middle portion, 4-6 months)
  • 2-3 year goals
  • Conservative investors wanting slight equity exposure

Example Funds:

  • ICICI Prudential Regular Savings Fund (XIRR: 9.93%)
  • Kotak Debt Hybrid Fund (XIRR: 11.63%)

Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Can outperform pure debt funds
  • Slight equity kicker with controlled risk
  • Suitable for medium-term goals
  • Better than FDs

Cons:

  • Equity exposure ‚Üí NAV volatility
  • Taxed as debt (less efficient)
  • Not suitable for very conservative investors

D. Arbitrage Fund

Investment Strategy: Hybrid-equity strategy that earns low-risk returns by exploiting price differences between cash and futures markets

How It Works:

  • Buy stock in cash market at ‚Çπ100
  • Simultaneously sell futures at ‚Çπ101
  • Pocket the ‚Çπ1 difference (arbitrage)
  • Very low risk as positions are hedged

Characteristics:

  • Risk: Very low (market-neutral strategy)
  • Returns: 7-7.5% XIRR
  • Volatility: Very low
  • Tax: Taxed as equity (major advantage!)
  • Credit Risk: None

Best For:

  • Short-term parking (3-12 months)
  • Travel fund corpus
  • Medical fund corpus
  • Emergency fund component
  • Alternative to liquid funds with better tax treatment

Example Fund: Kotak Arbitrage Fund (XIRR: 7.23%)

Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Taxed as equity (12.5% LTCG after 12 months)
  • Low volatility, market-neutral
  • Good for parking money 3-12 months
  • No credit risk
  • Better post-tax returns than liquid funds (for high tax bracket)

Cons:

  • Returns depend on market arbitrage spreads
  • Not ideal in extremely low-volatility periods
  • Slightly more complex product
  • Returns can vary month-to-month

The April 2023 Taxation Change

What Changed?

Before April 2023:

  • Debt fund LTCG (>3 years) taxed at 20% with indexation
  • Very tax-efficient for long-term investors
  • Could result in near-zero tax after indexation

After April 2023:

  • All debt funds taxed at slab rate (regardless of holding period)
  • No indexation benefit
  • No STCG/LTCG distinction

Impact

For High Tax Bracket (30%):

  • Debt fund returns reduced by 30%
  • Example: 8% return ‚Üí 5.6% post-tax
  • PPF (7.1% tax-free) becomes better
  • Bank FDs comparable (but less flexible)

Arbitrage Funds Became More Attractive:

  • Still taxed as equity (not affected by change)
  • LTCG 12.5% after 12 months
  • Much better post-tax returns

Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Treating Debt as Wealth Creator

Wrong: “I’ll become rich with debt funds”
Right: “Debt stabilizes my portfolio and funds short-term goals”

2. Ignoring Taxation

Wrong: Looking only at pre-tax returns
Right: Always calculate post-tax returns

3. All-in on One Debt Type

Wrong: Everything in liquid funds or everything in dynamic bond
Right: Mix based on time horizon and goals

4. Panic Selling During Equity Crash

Wrong: Selling debt during equity crash to buy more equity
Right: Debt is there to cushion, not to time equity

5. Ignoring PPF

Wrong: “PPF returns are too low”
Right: Tax-free 7.1% = 10%+ pre-tax equivalent for 30% bracket

Debt investments are boring. They should be boring. They’re not meant to excite you or make you rich.

Without debt, your equity journey will be too volatile to sustain. With proper debt allocation, you can ride out any market storm while building wealth systematically.
