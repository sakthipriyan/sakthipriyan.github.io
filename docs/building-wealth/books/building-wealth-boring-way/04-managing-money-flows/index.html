Managing Money Flows

Organizing Your Financial Life

The Core Idea

The secret to stress-free financial management isn’t earning more‚Äîit’s organizing what you have. Money separated by purpose, budgeted yearly, and allocated monthly creates clarity and control.

Each bank account or credit card serves a specific purpose, eliminating confusion and ensuring every rupee has a job.

Money Flow #1: Investments

The Investment Pipeline

Flow: Salary Account ‚Üí Investment Account ‚Üí Various Investment Vehicles

Investment Destinations

  1. Mutual Funds

    • Indian Equity Index Funds (Nifty 50, Next 50, Midcap, Smallcap)
    • Debt Funds (Liquid, Arbitrage, Hybrid)
    • Gold Mutual Funds

  2. International Broker Account

    • US Equity (NASDAQ 100 ETF)
    • Currency: INR ‚Üí USD conversion

  3. Gold Jewellery Scheme

    • Routed via Expense Credit Card (for rewards)
    • Payment from Investment Account

Monthly Investment Allocation

Automatic Process:

  1. Salary credited to Income Account
  2. Monthly allocation transferred to Investment Account
  3. SIPs/scheduled investments executed automatically
  4. Gold scheme installments paid via credit card
  5. Credit card bill settled from Investment Account

Key Principle: Investments happen first, before lifestyle expenses can consume the money.

Money Flow #2: Living Expenses

The Expense Management System

Flow: Salary Account ‚Üí Expense Account ‚Üí Multiple Credit Cards ‚Üí Actual Expenses

Monthly Allocation

  • Regular monthly expenses transferred from Salary Account
  • Predictable amount based on budget

Yearly Reserve

  • Allocated upfront in Salary Account
  • Used for annual/quarterly expenses (insurance premiums, subscriptions)
  • Transferred to Expense Account as needed

Expense Execution

Payment Methods:

  1. UPI from Expense Account: Immediate small payments
  2. Food Credit Card: All food delivery, dining, groceries
  3. Online Credit Card: E-commerce, subscriptions
  4. General Expense Card: Utilities, services, shopping

Settlement:

  • All credit card bills paid from Expense Account
  • Monthly review of spending patterns
  • Adjust allocations if needed

Why This Works:

  • Expense Account balance shows available budget at a glance
  • Credit cards provide 30-45 days float
  • Reward points add value
  • Clear spending categorization

Money Flow #3: Medical Expenses

The Healthcare Financial System

Flow: Salary Account ‚Üí Investment Account ‚Üí Medical Fund ‚Üí Medical Account ‚Üí Medical Expenses

Upfront Allocation

  • Monthly allocation for planned medical expenses
  • Separate to ensure immediate availability
  • No mixing with other expenses

Medical Corpus (Mutual Funds)

  1. Medical Fund (Arbitrage)

    • For planned medical expenses
    • Low-risk, good liquidity
    • Redeemed as needed

  2. Emergency Fund (Liquid + Hybrid)

    • For unplanned hospitalizations
    • Backup for insurance gaps
    • Larger medical emergencies

Medical Expense Handling

Payment Methods:

  1. Medical Account (via UPI/debit)
  2. Medical Credit Card (for larger bills)

Bill Settlement:

  • Credit card bills paid from Medical Account
  • Account topped up from Investment Account when depleted

Hospitalization Process

  1. Health Insurance: Primary coverage
  2. Medical Credit Card: Emergency spends, co-pays
  3. Medical Fund: Redeem and transfer to Medical Account
  4. Emergency Fund: If Medical Fund insufficient
  5. Investment Account: Final backup

Post-Emergency:

  • Rebuild Medical Fund first
  • Then restore Emergency Fund
  • Resume normal allocations

Money Flow #4: Travel Spends

The Travel Fund System

Flow: Salary Account ‚Üí Investment Account ‚Üí Travel Fund (Arbitrage) ‚Üí Travel Account ‚Üí Travel Expenses

Budget Planning

  • Yearly travel budget defined upfront
  • Monthly allocation accumulates in Investment Account
  • Invested in Arbitrage Fund for short-term growth

Travel Expense Execution

Pre-Trip:

  1. Estimate trip cost
  2. Redeem from Travel Fund (Arbitrage)
  3. Transfer to Investment Account ‚Üí Travel Account

During Trip:

  • UPI/Debit Card from Travel Account (local)
  • Travel Credit Card (preferred for rewards/forex)

Post-Trip:

  • Settle Travel Credit Card from Travel Account
  • Resume monthly allocations
  • Rebuild Travel Fund

Why Arbitrage Fund?

  • Short investment horizon (trips planned 3-12 months ahead)
  • Low risk, steady returns
  • Taxed as equity (advantageous)
  • Better than keeping idle in bank

Key Principles of Money Flow Management

1. Segregate by Purpose

Why: Clarity and control

  • Each account/card has one job
  • No mixing of purposes
  • Easy to track and manage

2. Budget Yearly, Allocate Monthly

Why: Predictability and discipline

  • Annual planning for big expenses
  • Monthly execution for consistency
  • No surprises

3. Automate Transfers

Why: Remove friction and emotion

  • Set up once, runs forever
  • No decision fatigue
  • Consistent execution

4. Use Credit Cards Smartly

Why: Leverage and rewards

  • 30-45 day payment float
  • Reward points and cashback
  • Better fraud protection
  • Easier expense tracking

5. Back Short-Term Needs with Liquid Assets

Why: Flexibility without risk

  • Travel Fund: Arbitrage Fund
  • Medical Fund: Arbitrage Fund
  • Emergency Fund: Liquid + Hybrid Funds
  • Not locked in equity or long-term debt

Implementation Guide

Starting Your System

Month 1: Assessment

  1. Track all current expenses for one month
  2. List all income sources
  3. Identify spending categories

Month 2: Setup

  1. Open necessary bank accounts (if not existing)
  2. Apply for purpose-specific credit cards
  3. Create budget allocations

Month 3: Automation

  1. Set up automatic transfers
  2. Configure SIPs for investments
  3. Link credit cards to accounts

Month 4+: Optimization

  • Review and adjust allocations
  • Fine-tune as needed
  • Maintain discipline

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  1. Too Many Accounts: Start simple, add complexity only if needed
  2. Irregular Transfers: Automate everything possible
  3. Raiding Investment Account: Only for true planned investments
  4. Credit Card Debt: Always pay in full, every month
  5. Ignoring Review: Monthly check-ins essential
