## What is Equity?

### The Basics

When you buy a **share** via **NSE** or **BSE**, you buy **a small piece of a company**. You become a part-owner of that business.

### How Equity Creates Wealth

Equity grows through multiple mechanisms:

1. **Price Appreciation**: As companies grow, share prices increase
2. **Dividends**: Regular profit distribution (if reinvested, compounds)
3. **Buybacks**: Company buying back shares increases remaining shareholders' value
4. **Stock Splits / Bonus Shares**: Increases number of shares you own

### The Non-Linear Nature

**Important**: Growth is never a straight line. Markets fluctuate based on:
- Company performance
- Economic conditions
- Investor sentiment
- Global events
- Interest rates

**Example**: Infosys share price

**Period**: 20 years  
**CAGR**: 12%

Despite 12% average annual growth, the path included:
- Years with 50%+ gains
- Years with 30%+ losses
- Sideways movement for 2-3 years
- Sudden spurts and crashes

**Key Learning**: Long-term holding smooths out volatility and captures growth.

### Price vs. Value

**Critical Understanding**: Prices change not every time value changes, but when **expectations** change.

- Company announces great results â†’ Price jumps (expectations raised)
- Economy slows down â†’ Price falls (expectations lowered)
- New product launch â†’ Price volatile (expectations uncertain)

Over long periods, price converges with actual business value.

## What is an Index Fund?

### Definition

An **Index Fund** is a **Mutual Fund** that buys all companies in an indexâ€”automatically.

### Key Characteristics

| Feature | Active Fund | Index Fund |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| **Management** | Fund manager picks stocks | Tracks index automatically |
| **Cost** | 1-2% expense ratio | 0.05-0.20% expense ratio |
| **Performance** | Tries to beat market | Matches market returns |
| **Holdings** | Concentrated (30-50 stocks) | Broad (50-500 stocks) |
| **Bias** | Manager's judgment | None, rule-based |

### The Power Equation

**Low Cost + Diversification + Discipline = Smart Investing**

### Why Index Funds Work

1. **No Guessing**: Removes the burden of picking winners
2. **Automatic Rebalancing**: Index changes â†’ fund adjusts
3. **Cost Efficiency**: More of your money compounds
4. **Emotional Discipline**: No temptation to time the market
5. **Transparency**: You know exactly what you own

### Step 4: Hold

**Time Horizon**: Minimum 10 years, ideally 20-30 years

**During This Time**:
- Markets will crash
- Markets will boom
- You will feel like selling
- You will feel like buying more

### Step 5: Rebalance (Optional, once a year)

Check if allocation drifted significantly:
- If equity grew to 90%, book some profit to debt
- If equity fell to 70%, add from debt to equity

**How Often**: Once a year, not more

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Trying to Time the Market

**Wrong**: "Market is high, I'll wait for correction"  
**Right**: Start SIP today, market timing doesn't matter long-term

### 2. Stopping SIP During Corrections

**Wrong**: "Market is falling, let me stop SIP"  
**Right**: Market falls = buying opportunity, continue SIPs

### 3. Chasing Past Returns

**Wrong**: "This fund gave 50% last year, let me switch"  
**Right**: Stick to index funds, ignore short-term outperformance

### 4. Over-Diversification

**Wrong**: 15 different equity funds  
**Right**: 3-5 index funds covering different segments

### 5. Frequent Portfolio Changes

**Wrong**: Reviewing and changing monthly  
**Right**: Review annually, change rarely
