## 7Ô∏è‚É£ When Active Funds Underperform ‚Äî The Hidden Cost of Exits (STCG/LTCG Impact)

One of the biggest challenges with **multicap and flexicap funds** is not just underperformance itself, but what it *forces investors to do*. When a fund starts lagging its benchmark for 1‚Äì3 years, most investors eventually lose patience and **exit**.

That exit triggers **taxes**, which permanently reduce long-term compounding.

### üîç The Two Layers of Damage

1. **Underperformance vs Benchmark:**  Active funds often deviate significantly from their benchmarks. When this happens for extended periods, switching feels justified ‚Äî but comes with a cost.

2. **Exit Taxes (STCG / LTCG)**  
   When you redeem units:  
   - **Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG)** ‚Üí *20% tax* if held < 1 year  
   - **Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG)** ‚Üí *12.5% tax* above ‚Çπ1.25 lakh gains  

   Even a seemingly small tax outgo every time you switch eats into your compounding base.

### üìå Why Index-Based Allocations Avoid This

With a rules-based index portfolio:  
- There is *no* expectation of ‚Äúmanager skill.‚Äù  
- Underperformance rarely triggers a switch, because indices naturally go through cycles.  
- Rebalancing is done through **cash flow**, avoiding taxable events.

This helps you stay invested, avoid emotional decisions and **prevent repeated tax leakage**.

### üß† Key Insight

Active investing has a structural disadvantage:  
> **Underperformance ‚Üí Investor exits ‚Üí STCG/LTCG ‚Üí Lower future compounding.**

Index-based allocation avoids this cycle entirely.
